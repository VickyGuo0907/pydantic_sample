The French Revolution began in 1789 and led to the end of the French monarchy, the establishment of a republic, and culminated in Napoleon Bonaparte seizing power in 1799. Key events include the storming of the Bastille on 14 July 1789.

The first moon landing took place on 20 July 1969 when Apollo 11 astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin landed on the Moon. Armstrong became the first human to set foot on the lunar surface.

World War II lasted from 1939 to 1945. It involved most of the world's nations and resulted in 70-85 million fatalities, making it the deadliest conflict in human history. It ended in Europe on 8 May 1945 (VE Day) and in the Pacific on 2 September 1945 (VJ Day).

The Great Wall of China was built over many centuries, with the most well-known sections constructed during the Ming dynasty (1368–1644). It stretches over 13,000 miles (21,196 km) across northern China.
