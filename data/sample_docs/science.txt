Photosynthesis is the process used by plants, algae and some bacteria to harness energy from sunlight and turn it into chemical energy. The overall equation is: 6CO2 + 6H2O + light energy → C6H12O6 + 6O2.

The speed of light in a vacuum is exactly 299,792,458 metres per second (approximately 3×10^8 m/s). Nothing with mass can travel at or faster than this speed.

Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius (212 degrees Fahrenheit) at standard atmospheric pressure (1 atm). The boiling point decreases at higher altitudes where atmospheric pressure is lower.

A leap year has 366 days instead of the usual 365. It occurs every four years, with the exception of century years that are not divisible by 400. A leap year contains 366 × 24 × 60 × 60 = 31,622,400 seconds.

DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) carries the genetic instructions for the development, functioning, growth and reproduction of all known organisms and many viruses. It is a double-helix polymer made of nucleotide units.
